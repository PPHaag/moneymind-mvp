Net Worth vs Deployable Capital

A high net worth does not automatically mean high financial flexibility. This lesson explains why usable capital matters more than most people realize.

Concept

Net worth is the total value of your assets minus your liabilities. It tells you how much wealth you own on paper. But deployable capital is different. It represents the capital you can actually access and use now.

Why it matters

Many people think they are financially stronger than they really are because much of their wealth is locked in pensions, property or illiquid assets. That means they may have wealth, but not flexibility.

Real-world example

Someone may have a home worth €500,000 with €150,000 in equity, but only €8,000 in cash. On paper this person has meaningful net worth, but in practice very little directly available capital.

Key insight

Net worth is not the same as deployable capital.

Reflection

How much of your current wealth could you actually use today if you wanted to make a financial decision, invest, or absorb a shock?
